The Science of Attention Economics

üß† What You Experienced

This game simulates real psychological and economic principles that tech companies use to capture your attention. Here's what's actually happening:

1. Attention as Currency: Your attention is literally worth money. Social media companies sell your attention to advertisers. The average person's attention is worth approximately $20-40 per month to tech platforms.

2. Variable Ratio Rewards: Apps use the same psychological tricks as slot machines‚Äîunpredictable rewards keep you coming back. You never know when the next interesting post will appear, so you keep scrolling.

3. The Time Distortion Effect: Research shows people underestimate time spent on social media by 50-75%. When you think you've spent 30 minutes, you've actually spent over an hour.

4. Cognitive Switching Costs: Every time you switch between activities, you lose 23 minutes of productive time on average as your brain refocuses. Constant notifications multiply this cost.

5. The Compounding Effect: Small daily losses in focus, sleep, and productivity compound into major impacts on academic performance, mental health, and long-term success.
